## The Rise of Express Air Cargo

Global e-commerce growth has fundamentally changed air freight demand. Consumers now expect two-day or even next-day delivery on international orders, placing enormous pressure on air cargo networks to deliver speed without sacrificing reliability.

## Capacity Challenges in 2024

The conversion of passenger aircraft belly capacity during the pandemic years created a temporary boom in freighter-only operations. As passenger travel recovered, belly cargo returned — but dedicated freighter demand remains strong for high-value and perishable goods.

Key pressure points this year:

- **Fuel surcharges** remain elevated, impacting cost calculations.
- **Geopolitical routing changes** are adding transit hours on certain corridors.
- **Peak season** (Q4) consistently sees 20–30% capacity tightening.

## When to Choose Air Over Sea

Air freight makes sense when:

- Your goods are **time-sensitive** (perishables, pharmaceuticals, electronics launches)
- The **value-to-weight ratio** is high enough to justify the premium
- You need to **replenish inventory** quickly to avoid stockouts
- **Sea freight disruptions** have caused delays that air can offset
