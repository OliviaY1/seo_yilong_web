## Why Customs Clearance Delays Happen

Customs delays are one of the most common and costly disruptions in international logistics. Most delays are preventable with proper preparation. Our compliance team has identified five areas where shippers consistently run into trouble.

## Tip 1 — Accurate Commodity Classification

Using the correct Harmonized System (HS) code is the single most important step. Misclassification leads to wrong duty rates, potential penalties, and holds. Cross-reference your product against the official tariff schedules of both the origin and destination countries.

## Tip 2 — Complete and Consistent Documentation

Every document in your shipment package must tell the same story. Inconsistencies between the commercial invoice, packing list, and bill of lading are an immediate red flag for customs inspectors. Double-check:

- Product descriptions (exact wording)
- Unit quantities and weights
- Country of origin
- Declared values in the correct currency

## Tip 3 — Pre-Clearance Where Available

Many countries now offer pre-arrival processing, allowing importers to submit documents before the vessel or aircraft arrives. This dramatically cuts dwell time at the port. Yilong files pre-clearance documents automatically for all regular customers.

## Tip 4 — Understand Restricted and Prohibited Items

Each country maintains its own list of goods that require special permits, are subject to quotas, or are outright prohibited. Failing to check these lists before shipping can result in seizure and non-recoverable costs.
